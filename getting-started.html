Getting Started

Start small and ship your first stream. Once the basics work, add polish over time.

Pick a goal

Decide what success looks like for the first stream.

  • Share a game with family.
  • Capture a single rink angle well.

Choose a path

Use a streaming service or build a simple DIY setup.

  • Services are faster to start.
  • DIY gives more control.

Minimum gear

Start with what you have and add one upgrade at a time.

  • Camera or phone with tripod.
  • Stable upload connection.

Test before game day

Run a short private stream to check audio and video.

  • Check lighting and framing.
  • Confirm stream stability.

Share responsibly

Communicate with teams and respect privacy requests.

  • Make takedown options visible.
  • Keep the focus on the players.

Tools I use

These are the tools behind my current workflow.

Streaming & Capture

  • OBS for live production.
  • Scoreboard for OBS for live overlays.
  • XbotGo for automated tracking, player tracking, and highlights (no subscription required).

Editing & Post

  • DaVinci Resolve for editing.
  • ffmpeg for fast exports.

Planning & Build

  • draw.io for diagrams.
  • Python for automation.
  • Terraform for infra.
  • Hammerspoon for global hotkeys.

Platforms & Delivery

  • Google Cloud for hosting.
  • Cloudflare for edge + DNS.
