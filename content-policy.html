Content Policy

This page explains what Streamn Dad records, shares, and publishes — and what we don't. For how we handle personal data, see our Privacy Policy.

What We Do

Streamn Dad livestreams youth and amateur hockey games and publishes highlight clips to social media platforms including YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, Threads, X, and TikTok. The goal is to celebrate the sport, share great moments with families who can't be at the rink, and help the community stay connected.

Where We Record

All recording takes place at ice rinks — public community facilities and privately owned venues that host games and tournaments open to spectators. These are the same environments where parents, grandparents, and fans already take photos and videos from the stands.

What We Share

  • Full-game livestreams and recaps
  • Highlight clips featuring goals, saves, and great plays
  • Tournament and game information (teams, scores, schedules)
  • Player names, jersey numbers, and team names from publicly available rosters and game sheets

What We Don't Share

Streamn Dad highlights are about celebrating the game. We intentionally leave out moments that could embarrass or single out any player, coach, or official:

  • No penalties or misconduct
  • No negative commentary on individual performance
  • No off-ice footage of players or families
  • No private or behind-the-scenes content without permission

Public Information

Player names, numbers, team names, and game details used in highlights and descriptions come from publicly available sources — printed game sheets, tournament websites, and rink scoreboards. This is the same information available to anyone attending or following the event.

No Opt-In Required — But Easy Opt-Out

Like any spectator with a camera at a public sporting event, Streamn Dad does not require participants to opt in to being recorded. However, we respect that not everyone is comfortable being on camera. If you would like a clip or livestream segment removed, we'll take it down — no questions asked.
